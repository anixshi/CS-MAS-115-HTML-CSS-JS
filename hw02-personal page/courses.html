Anika's Fall 2017 Schedule

When I'm not sleeping, watching TV, eating, or venturing into Boston, I'm in one of these classes: ARTH 100, CS 115, AFR 265 or LAT 200.

ARTH 100 with Prof. Greene

ARTH 100 is an introductory Art History class that explores issues like cultural appropriation, race, and gender. There's weekly lectures, readings, blog posts, as well as field trips required for the class. So far, I'm enjoying ARTH 100 because I'm being forced to rethink how I view and analyze art.

CS 115 with Prof. Metaxas & Prof. Pollalis

CS 115 discusses the "basic technologies" that allow for things such as Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram to exist. I'm also learning to write code in HTML, CSS, and (eventually) JavaScript. Though sometimes challenging, I love the sense of accomplishment I feel when I understand a concept or the code I write renders the way I expected.

AFR 265 with Prof. Cudjoe

In AFR 265, I get to read the autobiographies of well known African-American figures and explore the elements that makes up the African-American experience. It's a lot of reading and discussion (this class meets for 2.5 hours once a week) involved, but I enjoy reading about other people's lives.

LAT 200 with Prof. Starr

I took Latin in high school and liked it enough to want to continue studying it in college. In this class, we're reviewing all the major grammar and constructions of the language as well as reading Latin texts such as Res Gestae. Though continuing Latin in college has been enjoyable, I'm excited to be finishing up my language requirement.
